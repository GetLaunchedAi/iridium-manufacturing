Precision Waterjet Cutting


Versatile and Accurate Cutting Solutions


Iridium Manufacturing provides high-precision waterjet cutting services that offer a versatile alternative to traditional machining. Using a high-pressure stream of water mixed with abrasive particles, we can cut through thick and complex materials with extreme accuracy. Because waterjet cutting is a cold process, it eliminates the risk of thermal distortion or hardening, making it ideal for sensitive materials and complex geometries.



Our Waterjet Cutting Process

  1. Technical Consultation: Reviewing material specifications and design requirements
  2. CAD/CAM Integration: Programming precise cutting paths for optimal material usage
  3. Precision Cutting: Utilizing high-pressure waterjet technology for clean, accurate edges
  4. Quality Inspection: Ensuring all parts meet specified tolerances and finish requirements

Waterjet Cutting Capabilities

Waterjet Cutting FAQs

Common questions about our waterjet cutting process, materials, and advantages.

  • Waterjet cutting is a cold process, meaning it doesn't use heat. This eliminates heat-affected zones (HAZ), prevents material warping, and doesn't change the material's properties. It can also cut much thicker materials than most lasers.

  • Almost anything! We commonly cut stainless steel, aluminum, titanium, tool steels, copper, brass, composites, plastics, and even glass or stone.

  • Waterjets are capable of cutting materials from very thin foils up to several inches thick, depending on the material type and required precision.

  • Typical waterjet tolerances are around +/- 0.005", though this can vary based on material thickness and cutting speed. We work to your specific requirements.

  • Yes, waterjet cutting produces a smooth, satin-like finish that often requires no secondary deburring or finishing, saving time and cost.
